Our Story

In 2015, Francisco Uceda, a Spanish Language teacher at the Bronx High School of Science, started a Spanish Debate club in order to garner interest in the development of Spanish language outside of the classroom. He believed that true proficiency could only come if students challenged themselves and pushed themselves. Seeing the potential for debate as a method of sparking studentsâ€™ minds, he set out to incorporate the two. With several students, the program became a reality and the result is here today: The American Association for Foreign Language Debate. As shown through the name of the organization, he did not limit the program to only the Spanish language.
