Our Story

In 2015, Francisco Uceda, a Spanish Language teacher at the Bronx High School of Science, started a Spanish Debate club in order to garner interest in the development of Spanish language outside of the classroom. He believed that true proficiency could only come if students challenged themselves and pushed themselves. Seeing the potential for debate as a method of sparking students’ minds, he set out to incorporate the two. With several students, (names), the program became a reality and the result is here today: The Foreign Language Debate Association. As shown through the name of the organization, he did not limit the program to only the Spanish language.

Our Goal

Our goal is to educate and reinforce foreign language skills in a debate context. Most Americans are monolingual because they never had the opportunity to practice language skills outside of class. We want to change that by fostering a debate program that integrates the foreign language curriculum. We encourage non-native speakers to step outside of their comfort zone and utilize the skills they learn in class in these debates in order to reinforce the languages themselves.

How we do it

  • Train students and educators in the art of debate
  • Encourage development of foreign language through debate
  • Understand current issues in global contexts and discuss them in said foreign language
  • Organize, sponsor, and monitor debates
